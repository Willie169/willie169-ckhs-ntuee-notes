\sct{Lie Algebra}
\ssc{Matrix Lie group}
A matrix Lie group over $F$ is a closed subgroup of a general linear group over $F$.
\ssc{Lie group}
A Lie group is a topological group $(X,\tau,*)$ such that $(X,\tau)$ is a finite-dimensional smooth manifold, the maps of $(x,y)\in X^2\mapsto x*y$ and $x\mapsto x^{-1}$ are smooth maps.
\ssc{Lie Algebra}
A Lie algebra is a vector space $\mathfrak{g}$ over a field $F$ together with a binary operation $[\cdot,\cdot]\colon\mathfrak{g}\times\mathfrak{g}\to\mathfrak{g}$ called the Lie bracket, satisfying the following axioms:
\bit
\item Bilinearity:
\[[ax+by,z]=a[x,y]+b[y,z]\]
\[[z,ax+by]=a[z,x]+b[z,y]\]
for all $a,b\in F$ and $x,y,z\in\mathfrak{g}$.
\item Alternating property:
\[[x,x]=0\]
for all $x\in\mathfrak{g}$.
\item Jacobi identity:
\[[x,[y,z]]+[z,[x,y]]+[y,[z,x]]=0\]
for all $x,y,z\in\mathfrak{g}$.
\eit
\ssc{Lie algebra of a Lie group}
A Lie algebra of a Lie group is the tangent space at the identity.

\sssc{(Lie) exponential map}
Let $G$ be a Lie group and $\mathfrak {g}$ be the Lie algebra of $G$. The exponential of $X\in\mathfrak {g}$ is defined as
\[\exp(X)=\gamma (1)\]
where $\gamma \colon \mathbb {R} \to G$ is the unique one-parameter subgroup of $G$ whose tangent vector at the identity is equal to $X$.
