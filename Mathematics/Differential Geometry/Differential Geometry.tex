\ch{Differential Geometry (微分幾何)}
\sct{Riemannian Geometry}
\ssc{Curvature (曲率)}
\sssc{Plane curves}
Let $\gamma$ be a plane curve twice differentiable at $t$. The signed curvature $k$ of $\gamma$ at $t$ is given by
\[k(t)=\frac{\det(\gamma'(t),\gamma''(t))}{\|\gamma'(t)\|^3}.\]
The curvature $\kappa$ of $\gamma$ at $t$ is given by
\[\kappa(t)=|k(t)|.\]
For any $t$ such that $\kappa(t)\neq 0$, there exists a circle with radius $R(t)=\frac{1}{\kappa(t)}$ that is tangent to $\gamma$ at $t$.
